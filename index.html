This online toolkit includes resources from the Strategies for Sustainability workshop offered to CCPD/HDGP grantees in April 2018, and additional tools and templates to support sustainability planning. Sustainability encompasses much more than replacing an existing funding stream. Broadly speaking, sustainability refers to the capacity (e.g., resources and infrastructure) to maintain a program or service at a desired level to achieve specific outcomes or results.

Taking a comprehensive view of sustainability includes looking at financial needs and opportunities; considering options for delivery modifications or new partnerships; and policy, systems, and environmental change strategies.

Developing a sustainability plan can help solidify your organization or program’s goals and get buy-in from critical stakeholders and decision makers. A sustainability plan helps define short and long-term goals related to fund development, policy change strategies, and partnership building.

Strategies for Sustainability Workshop Materials

CCPD/HDGP grantees were invited to attend a half-day workshop focused on sustainability in Fort Collins, on April 5, 2018. During the workshop, CCPD/HDGP grantees had the opportunity to participate in a general session and breakout sessions that focus on sustainability strategies. The workshop was intended to support CCPD/HDGP grantees as they developed or refined a sustainability plan for their organization or program.

Workshop Learning Objectives

  • Describe what sustainability encompasses
  • Discuss two components of a sustainability plan
  • Practice applying sustainability strategies for their program/initiative
  • Identify 1-3 sustainability strategies for their sustainability plan

Bringing the Future Into Focus: A Step by Step Sustainability Planning Workbook

Bringing the Future Into Focus: A Step by Step Sustainability Planning Workbook is a comprehensive step-wise approach to planning for sustainability, starting at program inception. The workbook includes an easy-to-read guide, coupled with clear “your turn” activities to work through to create a comprehensive plan for your program or initiative. The workbook helps programs look at their sustained impact as well as direct program outcomes - i.e., the impact on partners, a system, or a community that may or may not be dependent on the continuation of the program or initiative. It also includes case studies of clinics and community-based organizations. This resource was developed by the Georgia Health Policy Center.
